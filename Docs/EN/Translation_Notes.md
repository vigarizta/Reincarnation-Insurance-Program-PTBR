# Translation Notes

## Methodology

The localization was produced as a full Brazilian Portuguese adaptation rather than a word-by-word conversion. Each entry was reviewed in context according to its namespace and function in the game: UI, equipment, abilities, events, dialogue, or update notes.

## Terminology Standardization

A glossary was maintained throughout the project to keep recurring terms consistent. Special attention was given to combat attributes, weapon types, equipment slots, divine technology terminology, status effects, UI labels, and progression terms.

## Manual Review

The translation went through editorial review focused on:

- Natural Brazilian Portuguese.
- Avoiding literal or mechanical phrasing.
- Consistent terminology.
- Clear gameplay meaning.
- Preservation of formatting, placeholders, numbers, RichText, and line breaks.

## Gameplay Testing

A standalone patch was created and tested in-game to confirm that the localization could be loaded without modifying the original game package. Visible UI areas were checked, including menus, settings, equipment-related text, and gameplay-facing labels.

## UI Consistency

UI strings were translated with readability and quick comprehension in mind. Short labels were kept concise, while descriptions were written to sound natural to Brazilian players.

## Dialogue Consistency

Dialogue and narrative lines were reviewed for tone, flow, and character voice. The objective was to preserve the game's personality while making the text sound native in Brazilian Portuguese.

## Equipment Consistency

Equipment names, weapon types, attributes, and descriptions were standardized to reduce ambiguity and help players understand item effects quickly.

## Localization Philosophy

The goal was to make the localization feel like an official Brazilian Portuguese release: clear, consistent, respectful of the original work, and comfortable for players to read during actual gameplay.
